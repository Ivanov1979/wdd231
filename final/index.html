Learning Objective

Add and subtract fractions with unlike denominators by creating equivalent fractions with a common denominator and explaining why the method works.

What you will see

  • Visual representations of fractions
  • Same-denominator vs unlike-denominator comparisons
  • Interactive practice with step-by-step solutions
  • Real-world word problems

Why a Common Denominator?

You can only add or subtract parts that are the same size. A common denominator means both fractions are divided into equal-sized parts, so the numerators represent the number of equal parts.
